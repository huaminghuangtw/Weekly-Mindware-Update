# Wisdom I Pondered This Week

* There are two types of people: (1) Those who want to know more (2) Those who want to defend what they already know
* Everyone wants to save the world, they just disagree on how.
* Focus on game access first, game selection next.
* Everything you're doing is meaningless—a few years after you're dead, no one's going to remember you or anything you've done. &harr; Everything you're doing is profoundly meaningful—even the smallest action is drenched with meaning. Remember to get out of your own head and appreciate that.
* There is no such thing as a Magic Certainty Button. Uncertainty equals reality.
* The Small-World Phenomenon indicates that an individual can be connected with any other in the world through a limited number of personal acquaintances.
* Never say never because limits, like fears, are often just an illusion.
* Cultivate a reputation for being dependable. Good reputations are valuable because they're rare (easily destroyed and hard to rebuild). You don't have to brew the most amazing coffee if your customers know the coffee will always be hot.
* Bored people are boring.
* Curiosity = What The F*ck Is That?
