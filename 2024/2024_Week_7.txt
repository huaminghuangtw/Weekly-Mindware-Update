# Wisdom I Pondered This Week

* The great use of life is to spend it for something that outlasts it.
* Our goal is to live so that we can confidently answer, "I didn’t miss it. I was there for it, and I invested my time and energy into the relationships that matter." This, to me, is the test of life.
* Some people come into your life as blessings. Other people come into your life as lessons.
* Having things isn't fun. Getting things is fun. → Becoming famous/wealthy is the most amazing feeling in the world. Being famous/wealthy is a mixed bag. Losing fame/wealth is a pain like you've never felt before.
* Think of your time as a jar. If you don't make space for the big rocks, the little pebbles and tiny sand will fill your whole jar. And you'll wonder why you're not getting anything meaningful done.
* Your job, throughout your entire life, is to disappoint as many people as it takes to avoid disappointing yourself.

# Life Pro Tips I Discovered This Week

* Count to 10 in your head before responding to a strong emotion to avoid regret.
* Send one message a day to a person you admire because you never know when you'll get a response back.
* Just because someone found a way into your email or DMs, doesn't mean you have to respond.
* If you need to calm someone down and get them to communicate, ask them questions about numbers or personal information.
* Instead of asking "Do you have any questions?", try "What questions do you have?"
* If you want your kids to listen when you have something important to say, speak very quietly. Yelling doesn't work, but a whisper grabs their attention.
* Swap out "I know" for "You're right!"
* Waiting 30-60 seconds to dry off after you turn off your shower significantly decreases the volume of water soaked up by your towel, thus your towel will need less time to dry by its next use.
